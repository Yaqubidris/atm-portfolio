Early Life and Education

Abdullahi Tijjani Muhammad Gwarzo was born on September 23, 1960, in Koyar Getso, Gwarzo LGA, Kano State. He began his education at Gwarzo Day Primary School, earning his Primary School Leaving Certificate in 1977. He proceeded to Science Secondary School, Dawakin Kudu, completing his Senior Secondary Certificate in 1982.

Gwarzo attended Ahmadu Bello University (ABU), Zaria, where he obtained a BSc in Textile Engineering in 1988. He fulfilled his National Youth Service Corps (NYSC) in Asaba Textile, Bendel State (now Delta State) between 1988 and 1989.

Political Career

Gwarzo started politics during Nigeria's Third Republic under the Social Democratic Party (SDP). He was elected Chairman of Gwarzo Local Government in 1991 and served until 1993. He returned to the position in 1997 under the United Nigeria Congress Party (UNCP).

In 2003, he was appointed Caretaker Chairman of Kano State ALGON and later became the State ALGON Chairman (2004-2007). He was appointed Special Adviser on Special Duties in 2007 before becoming Deputy Governor of Kano State from 2007 to 2011.

As Deputy Governor, he supervised the Ministry of Water Resources, leading major projects such as the Watari Waterworks and the Greater Kano Water Project. His dedication earned him the nickname "Ruwa Baba".
